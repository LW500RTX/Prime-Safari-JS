1 Sinharaja Forest Sri Lankan Leopard The Sri Lankan Leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya) occupies a distinctive place in the ecosystem. This elusive big cat, although not as frequently encountered as in some other habitats, adds an air of mystery to the rainforest. The dense canopy and diverse terrain of Sinharaja provide an ideal environment for the leopard's solitary and stealthy nature.
Despite the challenges of spotting these elusive creatures amidst the dense vegetation, the presence of the Sri Lankan Leopard underscores the ecological richness of Sinharaja. As a top predator, it plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of the rainforest ecosystem, regulating prey populations and contributing to the overall health of this UNESCO World Heritage Site. Conservation efforts within Sinharaja Forest aim to protect not only the leopard but also the entire intricate web of life that relies on the forest's delicate equilibrium. 		Deer
